What is a 0% intro APR credit card?

A 0% introductory APR credit card gives you a temporary period where you aren’t charged interest on either new purchases, balance transfers, or both. Once that period ends, your rate jumps to the card’s ongoing APR — which is often similar to what you’re paying today.

That intro window is your chance to make real progress. Instead of sending money to interest charges every month, more of your payment goes directly toward what you actually owe.

How long can you really avoid interest?

The exact length of the 0% period depends on the card. In our list below, you’ll see options with intro APR periods that can last well into 2026 on purchases, balance transfers, or both.

  • Some cards offer 0% intro APR on purchases only.
  • Others focus on balance transfers (moving an existing balance from another card).
  • A few cards combine both, but may have different timelines for each.

The key is choosing the card that matches your goal: paying off existing debt, financing a big purchase, or both.

When a 0% intro APR card can make sense

A 0% intro APR offer can be a smart move if you:

  • Have high-interest credit card debt you can reasonably pay down during the intro period.
  • Want to finance a large purchase and pay it off over time without adding interest.
  • Have a plan and budget in place to avoid running up new debt.

On the other hand, a 0% APR offer is not a solution if you keep spending more than you can repay. Once the intro period expires, your rate resets — sometimes to 25% or higher.

What to watch out for

Before you apply, review these fine-print details Important:

  • Balance transfer fee: Often 3%–5% of the amount you move. Run the numbers to make sure the interest savings outweigh this cost.
  • Deadline for transfers: Many cards require you to complete transfers within a set number of days to qualify for the promo rate.
  • Regular APR: Once the intro period ends, your rate will jump to a variable APR based on your credit.
  • Late payments: Paying late can cause you to lose the promotional rate and may result in fees or a penalty APR.

Used thoughtfully, a 0% intro APR card can be a powerful tool to get ahead of your debt. Used recklessly, it can leave you right back where you started — or worse.

How to use a 0% intro APR card to get ahead

Once you’re approved and your card is active, here’s a simple framework to follow:

  • 1. Add up your balances. Know exactly how much you’re moving and to which card.
  • 2. Calculate a payoff plan. Divide your total by the number of months in your 0% intro period to get a target monthly payment.
  • 3. Automate your payments. Set up automatic payments for at least the target amount.
  • 4. Pause new debt. Avoid swiping the old cards you just paid off — or you’ll end up with more debt, not less.

The goal isn’t just to “chase” 0% offers. It’s to use one well-timed intro APR window to make a permanent dent in what you owe.

Should you apply for more than one 0% APR card?

Some people spread their balances across multiple promo offers. That can work, but it adds complexity and risk. Multiple applications can temporarily impact your credit score, and juggling several due dates makes it easier to miss a payment.

For many people, starting with a single strong 0% offer and a clear payoff plan is a simpler — and safer — path.
